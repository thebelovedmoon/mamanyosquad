An Open Letter to Twitter

20 Nov 2022


Twitter has became the home of many opportunities that we don't know is possible. From memes to information about certain brands, and beyond -- it became the hub of what's known as the "Town Square of the People".

Of course, we won't even forget its mishaps and things that reshaped the platform throughout the years on a negative note -- drama, controversies, and even as far as exploitations that harm the users.

We just learned about what happened with the management a few days ago, and we are very optimistic at first with the new management -- only to realize the scathed reality of it being its own pile of mess because of demanding workflows and things that other employees think is not really necessary to implement in the first place. Despite this, we still kept on our optimistic approach that things would turn around despite those events.

During the course of these events that are currently unfolding, the public eye has already interpreted it as the "End of an Era for Twitter" as they deem know it. But on a personal level, we would say that we have grown much that we're establishing ourselves in our own right despite our accounts only being in the platform for a while. So, it's still in the best interest that we'll be staying on this platform while we work on other options.

But, there won't be an Open Letter like this without any demands and/or questions, so let's get down with it:

First off, why the layoff of the employees for such an overwhelming demand? If you're desperately trying to improve the services for the greater good of the people, then how come the employees are leaving en masse? Surely there's something wrong with the atmosphere, or -- as we've looked into the details as it unfolds -- it may be because of the demands that the employees can't stand it anymore.

Second, the verification procedures and how Twitter Blue is connected to it. We've seen users being verified because they're subscribed to Twitter Blue, which caused a lot of dismay for others. In other cases, some critics pointed out that people who are on the negative side of things (like being scrutinized and/or accused of certain allegations that critics see fit) that deter public's interest with certain topics are verified because of this change. We understand the case as to how it's implemented, but why? We're supposed to believe that notability plays a big factor in determining if we should get verified.

Third, management issues and recent receptions from the public eye. We're recently become aware that recent layoffs (as pointed in the first factor) caused an upheaval across the public, and they firmly believe that the upper management are to be blamed for all the chaos -- which also are underpinned by constant memes surrounding the "alleged" demise of the platform. Would you firmly believe it was the case? Are there any other options that could possibly turn things around for the better good? As far as we're concerned, we also don't want this legacy to perish because of such issues, but what can we do about it?

Fourth, combat to scrutiny. As we're writing this, there's already the news stating that the Former President that encouraged the insurrection against the White House and the Electoral College last 6 Jan 2021 -- Donald Trump -- will have his Twitter account reinstated back again by Mr. Musk. (By the time of issuance there's a high chance that it already has been done.) How would you -- as a company -- respond to such scrutinies, and how would you prevent such from happening that deter public interest -- without considering profits to boot? And what policies should be done in order to make this work?

And fifth, the continuous development of the platform following the mass exodus. As per our findings, if these events keep happening, there's a high chance that it will become underdeveloped and out-of-date -- security vulnerabilities (commonly referred as being CVEs), if left unpatched, may leave the platform compromised. It's understandable that more people are needed to combat such vulnerabilities, but with this event still ongoing, how will you combat such?

We understood that the new management will be tough, and we sympathize with you; but let's not forget that these (and other factors) exist and we need to maintain as much as we can in order for it to thrive further. Not to mention, having some adjustments to some features in response to the situation, but keeping some of the most important factors into consideration.

We firmly believe and hope that there's still some good way out without some of these overwhelming demands and mass exodus happening on every corner. We might not know what might that be, and what changes you might implement as a platform, but let's hope that the values we shared throughout the years remain the same.
